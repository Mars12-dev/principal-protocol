Principal protocol

1 Executive summary
Principal protocol is a proposed Soroban-based yield tokenization protocol for Stellar Real-
World Assets (RWAs). The mechanism follows the yield-tokenization principles demonstrated
by the well-established Pendle protocol, which separates a yield-bearing asset into two indepen-
dent instruments:
•Principal Token (PT): a fixed-income instrument redeemable at maturity for the prin-
cipal value of the underlying asset;
•Yield Token (YT): a tokenized claim on the future yield generated by the underlying
asset until maturity.
As an example, assume a user deposits a yield-bearing token worth 100 USDC and the maturity
is set in three months. The protocol splits this position into two instruments: a PT representing
the 100 USDC principal value, and a YT representing the yield generated over the three-month
period. If, at maturity, the underlying position is worth 103 USDC, the PT holder receives the
100 USDC principal value, while the YT holder receives the 3 USDC of yield. In this way, the
PT behaves like a fixed-income instrument, while the YT provides direct exposure to the future
yield of the asset.
The first supported market will be built onOndo USDY on Stellar. USDY is a tokenized
US Treasury-backed note and a yield-bearing asset, meaning that its value increases over time
as yield accrues. This makes it a natural candidate for PT/YT tokenization: conservative users
can buy PT-USDY to lock in a fixed yield, while yield-oriented users can buy YT-USDY to
express a view on future Treasury yield, secondary-market premiums or RWA adoption.
The project addresses a clear gap in the Stellar ecosystem. Stellar already has strong RWA
adoption, including Ondo USDY, Franklin Templeton BENJI, and Spiko USTBL. However,
Stellar does not yet have a dedicated yield-market infrastructure that allows users to trade, fix
and hedge future RWA yield. Principal protocol fills this missing layer.
2 Problem
Stellar has become one of the most relevant networks for tokenized RWAs, but most RWA assets
on Stellar remainpassive yield instruments. A user can hold USDY or other yield-bearing
RWAs, but cannot easily:
•Lock in a fixed yield before maturity;
•Sell future yield upfront;

•Express a directional view on future RWA yield;

Stellar has the underlying RWA supply, but not yet the yield market layer.
3 Solution
Principal protocol introduces a Soroban-based PT/YT protocol for Stellar RWAs. The initial
product will support USDY and create the following market:
USDY→SY-USDY→PT-USDY + YT-USDY
where:
•SY-USDYis a standardized yield wrapper that holds USDY and tracks its reference value;
•PT-USDYrepresents the principal claim, denominated in USDC accounting value;
•YT-USDYrepresents the yield generated by USDY between issuance and maturity.
At maturity, PT holders are entitled to the principal value, while YT holders are entitled to
the yield generated by the underlying USDY position. The accounting is done in USDC value.
Assume that the initial USDY position is worth 100 USDC. The protocol splits this position
into two instruments: PT-USDY, representing the 100 USDC principal value, and YT-USDY,
representing any value generated above this principal before maturity. The PT can then trade
below its maturity value. For example, a PT-USDY position redeemable for 100 USDC of value
at maturity may trade at 98 USDC before maturity. A user buying this PT at 98 USDC and
redeeming it for 100 USDC of value earns a fixed return of 2 USDC over the period.
If, at maturity, the underlying USDY position is worth 103 USDC, the PT holder receives 100
USDC of value, while the YT holder receives the remaining 3 USDC of value. The PT therefore
provides fixed-income exposure, while the YT captures the excess value generated by USDY
over the period. Because settlement is made in USDY, these USDC values are converted into
the corresponding amount of USDY using the USDY reference value at maturity. For example,
if 1 USDY is worth 1.03 USDC at maturity, then:
PT holder receives:100
1.03≃97.09 USDY
YT holder receives:3
1.03≃2.91 USDY
In this example, the full USDY position is worth 103 USDC at maturity: 100 USDC of value
is allocated to the PT holder, and 3 USDC of value is allocated to the YT holder. The PT
therefore receives the fixed principal value, while the YT receives the excess value generated by
USDY over the period.

4 Why this matters
4.1 For PT buyers
PT buyers are users looking for predictable fixed-income exposure from tokenized RWA assets.
Instead of holding a variable-yield asset directly, they can buy a PT at a discount and redeem
it at maturity for a known principal value. This is useful for users who want certainty on
their return profile, including treasury managers, stablecoin holders seeking fixed-term yield
and wallets or fintech applications that want to offer simple fixed-yield products to their users.
A PT is economically similar to a zero-coupon bond: it converts a variable-yield RWA position
into a fixed-term instrument with a defined maturity value. This makes PTs intuitive for fixed-
income users and particularly relevant for on-chain cash management.
4.2 For YT buyers
YT buyers are users who want direct exposure to the future yield generated by the underlying
RWA asset. Instead of buying the full USDY position, they buy only the yield component. This
allows them to express a view on future RWA yield, for example if they expect the realized yield
of USDY to be higher than the market-implied level. Therefore, YT can be useful for yield-
oriented users, active DeFi participants and protocols building structured products around
future RWA income. It creates a separate market for the yield component itself, while the PT
market serves users looking for fixed-income exposure. This separation allows different types of
users to access the same underlying RWA asset with different risk and return profiles.
4.3 For Stellar
Principal Protocol helps Stellar move beyond RWA issuance and custody toward deeper RWA-
based financial products. Stellar already supports major tokenized assets, but users currently
have limited tools to transform RWA yield into fixed-income products, yield markets or collateral
primitives. By introducing PT and YT markets, Principal Protocol turns tokenized RWA yield
into a more usable financial layer for Stellar DeFi.
The strategic benefits for Stellar are:
•deeper usage of Stellar RWAs;
•more on-chain financial activity around USDY and future RWA assets;
•new collateral types for Blend and other lending markets;
•stronger incentive for RWA issuers to deploy on Stellar;
•a differentiated RWA yield marketplace not yet available in the ecosystem.
5 Market validation from other chains
Yield tokenization has already been validated on major DeFi networks. Pendle, the leading pro-
tocol in this category, offers fixed-yield and future-yield markets across several chains, including
Ethereum, Arbitrum, Base, Optimism and BNB Chain. As of May 2026, Pendle has approxi-
mately$1.5b in TVL across chains, with active markets built on a wide range of yield-bearing
assets such as sUSDe, apyUSD, syrupUSDT and other DeFi yield products. This demonstrates
that users are willing to adopt a market structure where yield-bearing assets are separated into
fixed-income and future-yield components.

7 Why starting with USDY
USDY has been selected as the initial target asset for three main reasons.
1.It is a yield-bearing tokenized U.S. T-Bill product.
2.It already has meaningful market value on Stellar.
3.It is more composable than larger, more institutionally restricted RWAs.
It can then serve as the first market before expanding to other Stellar RWAs.
8 Technical approach
8.1 Protocol infrastructure
Principal Protocol will be built as a Soroban-native yield-tokenization and trading infrastructure
for Stellar RWAs. The first supported asset will be USDY, but the architecture is designed to
support additional yield-bearing RWAs. The design follows the yield-tokenization structure
demonstrated by Pendle: a yield-bearing asset is first standardized into a wrapper, then split
into a principal component and a future-yield component, and finally traded through a dedicated
PT/SY market. The protocol infrastructure is therefore composed of four main layers:
•Standardized yield wrapper: wraps the underlying RWA asset into a common yield-
bearing format. For the first market, USDY is wrapped into SY-USDY, which standardizes
deposits, redemptions, accounting value and reference-value tracking.
•Yield-tokenization engine: manages the creation, lifecycle, and maturity rules of PT-
USDY and YT-USDY. It defines how SY-USDY is split, how positions are recombined
before maturity, and how claims are settled at maturity.
4

•PT/SY liquidity market: provides the trading layer. The DEX is built around a single
PT-USDY / SY-USDY liquidity pool, which enables users to trade PT directly and trade
YT indirectly through routing, minting, and redeeming operations. This avoids separate
PT and YT pools, improves capital efficiency, and reduces liquidity fragmentation.
•Routing and settlement layer: abstracts the user flow across wrapping, tokenization,
trading, and redemption. Users can buy or sell PT and YT without manually manag-
ing each step, while the settlement layer handles maturity redemption based on USDC
accounting value and the USDY reference value.
8.2 Oracle and reference value module
The protocol needs a USDY/USDC reference-value oracle. Its role is to provide the accounting
value of one USDY in USDC. This value is required to price SY-USDY, settle PT-USDY and
YT-USDY at maturity, and determine the exact amount of USDY delivered to holders. Principal
Protocol needs a reliable reference value suitable for fixed-income accounting and redemption,
ideally coming from an issuer-provided feed or an institutional oracle provider.
8.3 Permissioned market design
Because USDY is a permissioned RWA asset, the first Principal Protocol market should also be
permissioned. Permissioning should apply across the full lifecycle of the market, from minting
and transfer to trading and maturity redemption, without requiring users to interact with
separate compliance steps at each stage. This design ensures that PT-USDY and YT-USDY
do not become a compliance bypass for restricted users. If the underlying USDY asset can only
be held or transferred by eligible participants, then the derivative instruments built on top of
USDY should follow the same logic. Principal Protocol will therefore enforce eligibility at the
protocol level while keeping the user experience as simple as possible for approved participants.
9 Business model
Principal Protocol will monetize through two protocol-level fee streams: a small fee on yield
claimed by YT holders, and swap fees on PT/SY trades. Fee revenue will support the protocol
treasury, fund ongoing operations, and finance incentive campaigns for users, notably liquidity
rewards for LPs in the PT/SY DEX pools.

12 Success criteria
Success will be measured by the delivery of a complete first USDY market on Stellar, from
testnet deployment to mainnet launch. The protocol should be live with one USDY matu-
rity, a functioning PT/YT trading market, permissioned access and tested maturity settlement.
Business success will be measured by the activation of both sides of the market. The first
market should attract PT/YT buyers and liquidity providers supporting the PT/SY pool. The
project should also secure active discussions or integrations with relevant Stellar ecosystem part-
ners, including wallets, oracle providers, RWA issuers, liquidity providers and lending protocols.
Ecosystem success will be measured by the ability to use the USDY launch as a foundation for
additional Stellar RWA markets. After the first market is live, the protocol should have a clear
expansion path toward other RWA assets and lending integrations.
13 Conclusion
Stellar already has significant RWA adoption, but it lacks a dedicated yield-market layer. Prin-
cipal Protocol brings PT/YT yield tokenization to the asset class where Stellar is strongest:
tokenized RWAs. By starting with USDY, the project targets a large, recognizable, yield-
bearing RWA already present on Stellar, while establishing a reusable infrastructure for future
RWA markets. The first release is deliberately focused: one asset, one maturity, permissioned
access, a clearly defined USDY/USDC reference-value oracle, and a controlled mainnet launch.
Once the USDY market is validated, the same architecture can be extended to other Stellar
RWAs. This provides a practical path to turn Stellar RWAs from passive yield-bearing assets
into programmable fixed-income markets.
